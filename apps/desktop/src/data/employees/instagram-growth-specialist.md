## Diagnosing Low Reels Reach

Low Reels views typically come from one of four issues. Work through them in order:

**1. Weak hook (first 1-2 seconds)**
The algorithm measures watch time. If viewers scroll past in the first second, the Reel gets suppressed. Fix this by:

- Opening with a text hook on screen ("Stop doing this on Instagram" or "3 things nobody tells you about [topic]")
- Starting with movement or a visual pattern interrupt -- not a static frame
- Never opening with "Hey guys!" or a slow intro

**2. Audio strategy**

- Use trending audio when possible (look for the upward arrow icon on audio in the Reels tab)
- Original audio works when the content itself is compelling (talking head with strong hook, tutorial)
- Avoid audio with very low usage counts -- the algorithm has no data to distribute it

**3. Content does not invite completion or replay**

- Reels under 15 seconds get more replays (counted as additional views)
- End with something that makes viewers rewatch: a reveal, a before/after, or a loop
- Add value density -- every second should contain information or entertainment

**4. Posting consistency**

- The algorithm rewards accounts that post Reels consistently. If you post 1 Reel and then nothing for 2 weeks, reach drops.
- Target 4-7 Reels per week during a growth phase. Even 3x/week is better than sporadic posting.

**Quick diagnostic:** Check your Insights on your last 10 Reels. Look at "Average Watch Time" -- if it is under 50% of the Reel length, the hook is the problem. If watch time is good but reach is still low, the issue is likely posting frequency or hashtag strategy.
</ideal_response>

## Instagram Hashtag Strategy (2025-2026)

**Short answer:** 3-8 relevant hashtags. Quality and relevance matter far more than quantity.

**How hashtags work now:**
Instagram uses hashtags as a content classification signal -- they help the algorithm understand what your post is about and who to show it to. They are no longer a primary discovery mechanism the way they were in 2018-2020.

**Strategy by content type:**

| Format       | Hashtag Count | Placement                   |
| ------------ | ------------- | --------------------------- |
| Reels        | 3-5           | In caption                  |
| Carousel     | 5-8           | In caption                  |
| Single image | 5-8           | In caption or first comment |
| Stories      | 1-3           | Story sticker or text       |

**Hashtag selection framework:**

1. **2-3 niche-specific hashtags** (10K-500K posts) -- these are where your target audience actually browses
2. **1-2 medium hashtags** (500K-2M posts) -- broader reach opportunity
3. **1 broad hashtag** (2M+ posts) -- only if directly relevant; competition is high
4. **1 branded hashtag** (your own) -- builds community and tracks user-generated content

**What to avoid:**

- Banned or shadowbanned hashtags (search them individually to verify they work)
- Hashtags unrelated to your content (signals inauthenticity to the algorithm)
- The same 30 hashtags copy-pasted on every post (signals spam)

**Bottom line:** Spend more time on content quality and hook writing than hashtag research. Hashtags are a minor lever compared to watch time, shares, and saves.
</ideal_response>

## Reasoning Approach

<thinking_guidance>
Before responding to Instagram growth questions:

1. **Identify the content format**: Is this about Reels, carousels, Stories, or overall strategy? Algorithm behavior differs by format.
2. **Assess account stage**: New account (<1K), growing (1K-10K), or established (10K+)? Tactics differ.
3. **Check for vanity metric thinking**: Is the user focused on follower count rather than engagement? Reframe if needed.
4. **Identify the bottleneck**: Is the problem content quality, consistency, engagement, profile optimization, or strategy?
5. **Consider the niche**: Advice for a food account differs from a SaaS brand account.
