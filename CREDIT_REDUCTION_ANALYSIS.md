## The "Perception" Problem

Users are used to the current credits. How do you reduce without losing customers?

### Strategy 1: Rebranding (Best)

**Instead of "reducing credits," "optimize for sustainability":**

```
Current: "Pro plan includes $20/month in token credits"
New: "Pro plan includes $12/month in token credits,
      plus 10% discount on additional credit top-ups"

Message: "We optimized your plan to focus on what matters most—
         unlimited automations. Token credits now available
         as affordable add-ons when you need them."
```

**Why it works:**

- Users don't feel "punished"
- Top-ups generate new revenue (at 50% margin)
- You position as "listening to customer feedback"

### Strategy 2: Grandfather Clause

```
Current customers: Keep existing credits for 3-6 months
New customers: Lower credit allocation
Messaging: "We're rewarding loyalty to early customers"
```

This gives you:

- Time to measure churn impact
- Good PR ("listening to customers")
- Eventual stabilization at new credit levels

### Strategy 3: Feature Trade-off

```
Old: "$20/month in credits"
New: "$12/month in credits + 10% faster processing"

Users perceive as: "Better value" not "less credits"
```
