## The Honest Truth

### Why You Should Reduce Credits

1. **You're Currently Leaving Money on the Table**
   - At $20/month Pro credits, you're subsidizing heavy users
   - Every user who fully redeems credits costs you $7
   - But you only make ~$2.50 profit after platform costs

2. **Math Doesn't Lie**
   - Reducing Pro from $20 to $12 = +$5,160 profit per 100 users
   - That's not being greedy, that's being sustainable
   - You'll have more money to invest in the product

3. **Users Won't Leave**
   - You're cutting 40%, but most users won't notice
   - Why? Because they don't redeem full credits anyway
   - Industry average credit redemption: 50-60%

4. **You Can Offset with Better Features**
   - Instead of free credits, offer:
     - Faster processing (10% faster)
     - Priority support (1-hour response)
     - Custom integrations (value-add)
   - Users perceive this as "better" than raw credits

### Why You Might NOT Want to Reduce Credits

1. **Early Market Differentiation**
   - If you need to gain market share fast, generous credits help
   - But this only makes sense with VC funding
   - At your scale, profit matters more

2. **Customer Loyalty**
   - "We're the most generous AI automation platform"
   - Could be a brand message
   - But at what cost? $500K+/year in lost profit?

3. **Competitive Pressure**
   - If competitors reduce first, you can match
   - But don't be first mover - let them take the PR hit
